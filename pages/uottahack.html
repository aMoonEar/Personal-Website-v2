Project Length - 8 months

Team Members - 2 Designers: Munir Aljawahari & Alistair McCarten

The official banner of uOttaHack 2020.

Context

uOttaHack is the University of Ottawa's largest ever hackathon, hosted by students for students of all studies across Canada. This year, uOttaHack 3 welcomed over 400 students from February 7th-9th to collaborate on projects, learn at workshops, and develop their innovative ideas. The uOttaHack committee decided to launch a rebrand that will represent this special uOttaHack and continue to represent the face of uOttaHack for years to come.

Previous Design

In previous years, uOttaHack was struggling to have a consistent design. In 2018, uOttahack..... In 2019, uOttaHack adopted a trendy isometric illustration theme to appeal to sponsors and hackers. We wanted to give uOttaHack a new logo and theme that would remain on trend throughout the years sustain through trends but also adapt to seem different every year.

Comparison of the 2018 homepage (left) and 2019 homepage (right) for uOttaHack.

Purple was the base color for most designs. It was a dark color that allowed flexibility when choosing a secondary color to include. The addition of orange and peach to the color palette provided the much needed contrast. Lastly, the blue provided the needed transition between the purple and orange.

The illustrations that were used were taken from paaaaterns. We felt that the illustration style complimented the color scheme that was already defined In addition, illustrations from humaaans, and the public humans library, and whooosh. designing swag I worked with my design partner Alistair to design physical assets that would be given to hackers during the hackathon. All swag was made to conform to the style guide that we curated.

Graphics in addition to swag, I also had the responsibiliy of creating many social media assets for facebook, instagram, linkedin. These assets included graphics such as promotional content, countdown posts, as well as posters that would be hung around campus (however did not get printed due to time constraints)

takeaways a rebrand is not easy i did not expect the rebrand to take the most of the design process. it took a long time because it would serve as the foundation for all the work that would stream into the brand spend time into making a thorough style guide i underestimated the value of a style guide, especially when working on a team. luckily, i only had one partner, however, i see how important it is when working on a large team. it prevents mistakes being made, and ensures all designers are on the same page. i hope to keep designing in large groups to continue my group contribution skills.
