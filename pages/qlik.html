Qlik Internship

Role - Cloud Backend Developer
Responsibilities - Redesign brand, logo, website and merch
Contract Length - 4 months
Team Size - 2 managers and 6 developers

What is Qlik?

In the first four months of 2020, I had the privilege of working for Qlik in Kanata, Ottawa. Qlik is a company that specializes in making business intelligence software, and the office in Kanata was for the online Saas (software as a service) component.

What did I work on?

I worked under the Identity Management Team that was responsible for developing authentication, authorization, and permissions throughout the product. I was later given the opportunity to be a scrum master to lead the team as a couple members were on leave.

What I learned about software

My learning curve at Qlik was very steep as this was my first time working in a private company. Here are some of the few things I mastered during the four months I worked at Qlik.

  • The first thing I learned was how to effectively use terminal to operate micro-services and contribute to github

  • How to build and use APIs to transfer important data between microservices

  • What HTTP status codes are and their significance when testing backend infrastructure

  • How to use Node.js and Go to develop microservices and deploy them in Docker and Kubernetes

  • Wrote simple unit and component tests to maintain stability in a service

  • I learned what it meant to write simple code and how to meaningfully document it

What I learned about agile

I loved the agile methodology of management when I was first introduced to it. I felt that it enabled each person to work their best while also allowed each member to clearly communicate their blockers.

  • A scrum master is more than just running scrum meetings, it is more about paying attention to the team’s daily activities and knowing everyone’s strengths and weaknesses

  • Preparedness and keeping meetings short is important to keep team members engaged

  • Communicating the expectations and goals helps keep the team on track

Takeaways

I want to thank Qlik for the great opportunity and for allowing me to act as a scrum master despite working remotely during April due to COVID-19.

I realized that the time and relationships I developed with my co-workers were not to be taken for granted. Many employees at Qlik made me feel very welcomed and have mentored me when not many other companies would do so.

I also learned that it is much harder to connect with members of the team remotely than in person. Remotely it can be much more difficult to spin casual conversations to develop meaningful relationships with employees. On many days the only time I got exposure to my teammates was during our daily stand-up meetings.

Overall, I hope to continue expanding my experiences with the knowledge that I gained at Qlik.
