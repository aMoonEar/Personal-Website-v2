Why I'm the right person for this role

I am the right person for this role because of my dedication to my craft and my positive attitude to the workplace.

My dedication to Web Development began in high school with simple HTML/CSS through codecademy. Soon enough, I was mastering Bootstrap and Javascript which allowed me to today create munir.xyz, my personal portfolio website. This confidence led me to participate in hackathons such as CANDEV Government of Canada hackathon (which I won WOO!) and helped me get my first internship at Health Canada.

Although there weren’t many software developers at Health Canada, I still learned as much as I could and implemented new software into their practices. I ended up using my expertise to recreate an old PDF form into an interactive webpage, and created a fully custom Drupal website to aid project management, both of which were huge successes! Employees at Shopify always look for ways to make an impact, and I wanted to make as big of an impact as I could within my internship, which helped me secure a part-time role with them during school.

After a successful internship at Health Canada, I was able to work for the large private company Qlik. Attaining that internship was a big step in making me the right person for this role. During my time working at Qlik, I learned about Lodash and other libraries, the importance of writing strong unit and integration tests, back-end structure (middleware, models), and so much more. I came into Qlik on my first day unsure of what SaaS models were, and left being able to run scrum meetings and deploy and maintain microservices myself.

While having technical abilities are important, I believe what sets me apart from the rest is my positive attitude and collaboration skills. At Qlik, I had a good relationship with other developers because I kept a positive attitude towards learning, never hesitated to ask questions, and always sought bigger opportunities to prove myself.

This led me to become a scrum master as an intern, where I became responsible for running every agile meeting building a positive atmosphere. Not to mention, I had to make quick decisions based on the needs of my manager (such as expecting us to stop all of our exciting feature work to barrage the endless stack of bugs). To be a scrum master, not only do you have to unblock the team, but I had to understand every aspect of Qlik’s product. I loved exploring potential problems for tenants, and I hope to one day spread that love for finding and solving merchant issues to Shopify.

All in all, I believe my dedication to development and design combined with my desire for positive impact in a team setting is what sets me apart from other candidates.
